# Podcast publication (solo founder / operator / B2B)

Load this reference when drafting a podcast episode (solo or interview), show notes, episode title, episode description, or full publication package.

- **YouTube announced 1 billion monthly podcast viewers** in early 2025 and **launched its Weekly Top Podcast Shows chart on May 15, 2025** (ranked by watch time, not downloads).

- **Global podcast ad revenue ~$5B in 2026**, up ~20% YoY (Deloitte).

## Episode architecture: solo vs. interview

### Solo episode "narrative arc" (the dominant 2026 pattern)

- **Cold open (30–90 seconds):** the most-clipped moment from the episode, lifted out and placed before the intro music. **No longer optional** — every successful operator podcast in 2026 (Lenny's solo episodes, Stratechery's audio, BG2, Acquired ACQ2) does it.
- **Intro music sting (5–15 sec):** branded.
- **Setup (1–3 min):** problem, why it matters, why you specifically.
- **Body, three-beat structure (15–35 min):** problem deepening → tension/conflict → resolution. Heavy signposting ("First… second… now here's where it gets interesting…").
- **Takeaway / one-liner (1–2 min):** the line listeners will quote in their group chats.
- **CTA (30–60 sec):** one ask. Newsletter signup, share, leave a review — pick one.

### Interview episode prep

- **Question bank:** 12–18 questions written in advance, of which you'll use 6–9. Always more than you need.
- **The "3-question rule":** the first three questions should establish credibility (a question only this guest can answer), set up the spine of the episode, and produce the cold-open clip. Lenny Rachitsky and Patrick O'Shaughnessy both follow versions of this.
- **Transition planning:** write your three to five "if-they-go-here-I-go-there" pivots in advance. The amateur tell is reading questions in order; the professional tell is conversational pivots that still hit your spine.
- **Pre-written cold open:** record a teaser AFTER the interview, not before. Use the strongest 45–60 sec clip from the actual conversation.

### Writing for the ear, not the page

- No em-dashes (you can't pronounce them).
- No parentheticals.
- Short sentences. Read your script aloud — if you stumble, rewrite.
- Repetition is helpful, not lazy. Listeners aren't re-reading.
- Signposting is essential: "There are three things to know here. First…"
- Numbers spoken aloud should be rounded ("about 30,000," not "29,847").

## Show notes craft (dominant 2026 format)

There are now **two coexisting standards**:

### (a) "Summary + bullets + timestamps + links + bio" — the classic format (300–800 words)

Works for Apple Podcasts/Spotify display. Apple caps show notes at **4,000 characters**; Spotify (and most apps) collapse after **~100 characters** with a "see more." So the **first 1–2 sentences must hook + summarize**.

### (b) "Full essay show notes" — the Lenny / Stratechery / Newcomer pattern

The rising 2025–2026 format. Show notes function as a **standalone blog post on your website** at 1,500–2,500 words, optimized for E-E-A-T and Google podcast search. The web version is substantively different from the in-app version. **What's winning for SEO discovery.**

### Recommended 2026 stack

1. **In-app show notes (Apple/Spotify):** 200–500 words. Hook in line 1, 3–5 bullet takeaways, timestamps, 3–5 links, guest bio, one CTA.
2. **Website show notes (your own domain):** 1,500–2,500 words. Treat as a blog post. H2s/H3s targeted at search queries, embedded player, structured data (PodcastEpisode, AudioObject, FAQPage JSON-LD), full edited transcript at the bottom.
3. **Apple's auto-transcript:** since March 2024 Apple generates this automatically. Best practice in 2026 is to **upload your own polished VTT** (improves accuracy, accessibility, and Apple in-app search ranking).

**The "preview snippet" rule:** Apple/Spotify/Overcast all show ~100–200 characters before the "See more" cut. Front-load the promise:

> "How Stripe's first 50 hires were chosen — including the bar-raiser test that nearly killed the company. With co-founder John Collison."

## Episode title craft

**Three patterns that work in 2026:**

- **"Guest Name: Specific Promise"** (Tim Ferriss / Lenny school) — "Lenny Rachitsky: How to spot a top-1% startup early."
- **"The Topic Statement"** (NYT The Daily / Stratechery school) — "The end of the seed round."
- **"How [Outcome]"** (operator/founder show school) — "How Notion got to 100M users."

**Length:** Apple Podcasts cuts at ~80 chars on mobile; Spotify ~60. **Aim for 50–70 chars** in the title field. Anything over 80 is invisible on mobile in most clients.

**2026 trend: episode numbers are leaving titles.** Lenny's, Acquired, Stratechery, BG2 have all dropped the "#147 –" prefix. It eats characters and signals to new listeners that they're "behind." Keep episode numbers in the metadata field, not the title.

**Banned in 2026:**

- Vague "On X" titles ("On the future of work") with no specific promise.
- Clickbait that doesn't deliver — reviewer culture on Apple/Spotify is fast to call this out.
- Uninformative guest-only titles ("My conversation with Sam") on shows that aren't already huge.

## Episode description / in-app notes

**Structure:**

- **First 200 chars (hook + promise):** the visible preview.
- **Body (proof):** what specifically is in this episode, who the guest is, why it's worth their hour.
- **Timestamps** (also act as YouTube chapters if simulcasting).
- **Mentioned tools/books/brands** (helps discovery — listeners search these names).
- **Links:** 3–5 max. Limit dramatically.
- **CTA:** one.
- **Hashtags don't work in podcasting.** Keywords in description text help Apple in-app search (especially since the 2024 transcript indexing). For some directories (Goodpods, Podchaser) tags help.

## Algorithm and distribution mechanics

**Apple Podcasts** ranks by category + region with recency weighting. The old "hockey-stick gaming" of the New & Noteworthy charts via burst-day downloads has been mostly neutralized since 2023. Apple's discoverability in 2026 is driven by: (a) consistent listening velocity, (b) auto-transcript indexing in supported languages, (c) listener actions (subscribes, follows, episode shares).

**Spotify** is fully algorithmic and personalized via the user's listening graph. Spotify added a **paid-promotion boost lane** in 2025 for shows that pay to be surfaced in "More like this" rails. Spotify Q&A and Polls features (rolled out 2023, expanded 2024) are explicit ranking inputs — episodes with Q&A activity get re-surfaced. Spotify's **Comments** feature (launched 2024, auto-publish expanded 2025) is now a meaningful retention signal.

**YouTube** is the largest discovery surface — see `references/youtube-long-form.md` for mechanics. YouTube's Weekly Top Podcast Shows chart ranks by **watch time**, not downloads.

**Podcast SEO via on-website show notes** is the third growth channel. Dedicated episode pages with FAQPage schema, full transcripts, and 1,500–2,500-word summaries can rank in Google for long-tail queries within 4–6 weeks of optimization. ~40% of new listeners discover shows via in-app or web search.

## The video podcast reality (2025–2026)

**Should every solo founder/operator podcast also be on YouTube?** In 2026, effectively yes — at minimum as static-image audio or simple two-camera setup. Cost-benefit:

- **Cost (per a 2025 Podcast Marketing Academy / Lower Street study):** producing video alongside audio costs **+77% more** and requires **~50% more staff** than audio-only.
- **Benefit:** YouTube is the #1 discovery surface. Among Gen Z, 84% of new podcast listeners discover shows via YouTube. Per Acast's 2025 *Podcast Pulse*, audio remains the trust/connection medium; video drives reach.

**Practical workflow that works for solo operators:**

1. Record on Riverside or Descript (16:9 video, separate audio tracks).
2. Edit one master in Descript.
3. Export three deliverables: full audio (RSS), 16:9 long-form video (YouTube), 9:16 vertical clips (Shorts/Reels/TikTok, 3–6 per episode).
4. Static-image audio version for YouTube is acceptable as a starter — Spotify and YouTube both show audio-only as "podcast" content if metadata is mapped.

## Monetization in 2026

**The CPM-only model is dead for shows under ~50,000 downloads/episode.** 2025 industry benchmarks:

- Pre-roll: $15–$30 CPM
- Mid-roll host-read: $25–$40 CPM (premium niches: $40–$75)
- Post-roll: $10–$20 CPM
- Programmatic non-host-read: $5–$15 CPM

A finance show with 10,000 downloads selling one mid-roll at $30 CPM = $300/episode. That's not a business. Shows under 50k downloads in 2026 win with:

- **Single-sponsor exclusivity** (one brand per quarter; flat $5k–$25k deals; relevance > volume).
- **Audience-direct sponsorship** — your listeners' employers buy slots.
- **Substack / Beehiiv / Ghost paid bonus episodes** — the dominant model for operator/founder shows.
- **Patreon / Apple Podcasts Subscriptions / Spotify Subscriptions for Podcasters** for paid feeds.
- **Indirect monetization** (course, consulting, SaaS) — "the show is the funnel" pattern that converts at consulting prices ($5k engagements vs. $25 CPM impressions).

**Dynamic ad insertion (DAI)** is now standard at hosting platforms (Buzzsprout, Megaphone, Captivate, Castos). Allows back-catalog monetization, ad swapping, geo-targeting. Most modern podcast advertising is dynamic in 2026.

## Compliance (regulated verticals)

- **Financial podcasts:** explicit "not investment advice, for informational purposes only" verbal disclaimer in cold open or intro, repeated in show notes. FTC-compliant affiliate disclosure for any code/link mentioned ("This is a paid promotion / I receive a commission"). For US: SEC's marketing rule applies if the host is a registered IA. UK/EU: FCA "not a financial promotion" language.
- **Crypto:** SEC/FCA "not financial promotion" language plus "I may hold positions in tokens discussed."
- **Medical/health:** "not medical advice, consult a clinician." HIPAA does not apply to general commentary but does to any patient case discussions.
- **Legal:** "not legal advice, no attorney-client relationship is formed."

## AI-content in podcasting

**2024–2025 saw a flood of AI-generated podcasts** (NotebookLM-style, ElevenLabs voice-cloned shows). **Listen Notes built a "NotebookLM Detector" in 2024** and flagged 280+ shows; founder Wenbin Fang publicly called them "a threat to the podcasting community."

**Platform stances:**

- **Apple Podcasts:** no formal blanket ban; relies on general spam/quality policies. AI-narrated shows that are mass-produced or low-effort can be removed under existing policies.
- **Spotify:** allows AI shows but actively promotes human-hosted shows in editorial picks; AI music has been more aggressively policed than AI podcasts.
- **Podcasting 2.0:** an emerging community proposal for a generative-AI disclosure tag in RSS namespaces. Voluntary as of early 2026.

**Community sentiment** among genuine operator/founder podcasters has hardened against AI-cloned voices specifically — analogous to the Bluesky anti-AI-art stance.

**Best practice in 2026:** if any portion of an episode uses AI-generated voice, AI-generated commentary, or AI-cloned voice, disclose it explicitly in the cold open ("the voice you're hearing in the next segment is AI-generated") and in show notes. NotebookLM-style automated podcasts should be clearly labeled — failing to do so is a reputational risk that tanks reviews.

**AI for production assistance** (transcription, editing, filler-word removal in Descript, show-notes drafting, clip selection) is universally accepted and not disclosed.

## Hooks/openings that work in 2026

**The cold-open teaser** (lift the most-clipped 30–90 sec moment from the interview, place it before the music) is the dominant 2026 opener. It works because:

- The first 60 seconds is the leading indicator of completion rate (per Spotify and Apple internal data referenced across 2025 industry reports).
- It self-selects for listeners who want this episode specifically.
- It produces shareable clips for Shorts/Reels/TikTok automatically.

**Patterns that work:**

- **Receipts cold open:** "We crossed $10M ARR in 18 months. Here's the exact channel mix that got us there. With Mariah, founder of [company]."
- **Contrarian-quote cold open:** Lift the guest's strongest contrarian line, play it raw, then say: "Today on the show — [name] on why [conventional wisdom] is wrong."
- **Narrative cold open** (Acquired/Stratechery): "In 1987, a 24-year-old engineer turned down a job at Apple. Twelve years later, that decision created a $200B company. Today on Acquired…"

## Banned phrases / AI-tells (podcast culture)

- "Welcome to the show" as the very first words (kills the cold open).
- "Without further ado, let's get into it."
- "First and foremost…"
- "At the end of the day…"
- "It's important to note that…"
- AI narrator pacing on numbers, names, or unusual words (gives away TTS).
- Identical sentence rhythm across the entire intro (a giveaway of AI-written script, even when read by a human).
- Chapter-marker timestamps that don't match the actual content (audiences notice in 2 episodes).
- "Hit subscribe and leave us a 5-star review" without context — the social contract is now "earn the review."

## Verdict

Podcasting in 2026 is a video-first, narrative-first, evergreen-search-discoverable medium. Audio-only is no longer competitive on discovery; weekly chat shows are flatlining; cold-open teasers are mandatory; full-essay show notes are the SEO play. Pair the show with a newsletter or paid product — the show alone is rarely a business under 50k downloads.
