# YouTube long-form (the full upload package)

Load this reference when drafting a YouTube long-form video script, title, thumbnail copy, description, or full upload package.

## Algorithm and distribution mechanics (2026)

YouTube no longer runs "an algorithm." It runs **five separate recommendation systems** that share core signals but rank differently: Browse (Home feed), Suggested, Search, Subscriptions, and Shorts. Long-form videos primarily live or die on Browse and Suggested.

**2026 ranking signals, in order of weight:**

1. **Satisfaction signals** (post-watch surveys, repeat viewing, rewatch, session continuation, comment sentiment) now outweigh raw watch time. Headline 2025–2026 shift, confirmed by YouTube's growth team and CEO Neal Mohan.
2. **CTR on impressions.** Healthy: 4–10% most niches; 2–6% educational/B2B; 8–15% entertainment. CTR alone is no longer enough — must pair with retention.
3. **Average view duration (AVD)** and **average percentage viewed (APV)**. A 6-min video with 80% retention beats a 20-min video with 30% retention.
4. **Session contribution** — does watching your video lead viewers deeper into YouTube vs. closing the app? Outbound link clicks and "Not Interested" presses are negative signals.
5. **Topical/channel authority** — YouTube increasingly evaluates channels, not just videos. Niche consistency compounds; channels that drift get deboosted on Browse.
6. **Subscriber/notification engagement quality** (not raw count). Bell-on viewers who actually watch new uploads weight heavily.

**Critical 2025–2026 architectural changes:**

- **Shorts and long-form algorithms are fully decoupled** (late 2025). Shorts performance no longer drags down or lifts long-form on the same channel. Different growth channels, different signals.
- **Browse personalization moved from broad topic categories to viewer "watch history clusters"** — niche content is more visible than in 2023–2024.
- **Title + thumbnail A/B testing expanded December 9, 2025** to allow up to three combinations of titles, thumbnails, or both, optimized on **watch time per impression** (not CTR) over up to a two-week window.
- **Hype** (a viewer-driven push for sub-500K-subscriber channels onto regional leaderboards) is an active 2025 distribution lever.
- **Neal Mohan's 2026 priority is managing "AI slop"** — the algorithm performs deeper content analysis on visuals, audio, and speech to assess originality.

**Browse vs. Suggested vs. Search rule of thumb:**

- **Browse:** wins on packaging (title + thumbnail + topic-of-the-moment) and channel authority.
- **Suggested:** wins on co-watch patterns and same-creator/same-topic relevance.
- **Search:** wins on title/description/spoken-content match to query plus retention from search traffic. Tutorials and evergreen content disproportionately rank here.

## Format and length

The dominant 2026 long-form length is **8–18 minutes**, with a long tail of 20–60-min documentary/deep-dive content (Acquired, Karpathy, Asianometry) and a separate cluster of fast-cut 4–8-min Fireship-style explainers. The 10-minute mid-roll ad eligibility threshold has not changed.

For B2B/founder/operator/expert content: **12–18 minutes is the sweet spot.** Long enough for mid-rolls; short enough that 50% retention is achievable.

## Script architecture — three working structures for 2026

### (a) The "stair-step" hook + escalation structure (MrBeast-derived)

For a 12-min video:

- **0:00–0:08 (the "5-second hook"):** the most arresting visual + verbal claim. No logo, no music sting, no "Hey guys."
- **0:08–0:30:** stakes statement + payoff promise ("By the end you'll know X, and I tested it on Y.")
- **0:30–3:00:** "crazy progression" — first concrete payoff arrives by the 1-min mark; second by 2 min. MrBeast's rule: "the first minute is the most important; the next two transition from hype to execution."
- **3:00–10:00:** stair-step — each segment raises stakes. ($1 yacht → $1M yacht → $1B yacht.) For B2B: claim → small proof → bigger proof → biggest proof → counterintuitive twist.
- **10:00–11:30:** the resolution / data dump.
- **11:30–end:** tight CTA (one ask), no rambling outro.

### (b) The "open loop" structure (Veritasium / educational)

Open with a counterintuitive question creating cognitive dissonance, refuse to answer for 8–12 minutes, deliver the resolution at the end. Working 2024–2026 openings: "Why do all these shapes make the same shadow?" / "90% of startups fail — but not for the reason you think."

### (c) The educational tutorial three-act

- **Act 1 (0:00–2:00):** problem statement + why most solutions fail.
- **Act 2 (2:00–9:00):** the actual method, demonstrated step-by-step.
- **Act 3 (9:00–12:00):** edge cases, common failures, the result.

Ali Abdaal, Tiago Forte, and most creator-economy tutorial channels run this.

### B2B / founder differences

- Cold open is usually **a specific result + a specific stake** ("I spent $40k on Google Ads for one SaaS in 90 days. Here's exactly what worked, what didn't, and the dashboard."), not a personality-driven hook.
- The intro card / channel logo, if used, lands at 0:08–0:15, never before the hook.
- Greg Isenberg, Pat Walls (Starter Story), Marc Lou run **documentary-style "build-in-public"** structures: episode-style "Day 1 of 30," real revenue dashboards on screen, sub-2-second cuts when on-camera, b-roll of the actual product/screen.
- Fireship runs ultra-condensed 4–6 min with hyper-paced editing — works because value density per second is extreme.
- Karpathy, Asianometry, Lex-Fridman-style 60min – 4hr long-form works **only** with a pre-existing audience; not a starter strategy.

## Hooks/openings that work in 2026

- **Stair-step hook (claim → stakes → proof → promise):** "Last month I shipped a SaaS in 14 days. It made $2,400 in week one. Here's the entire codebase, the launch tweet, and the three mistakes that almost killed it before launch. Do not do what I did at minute 4."
- **Cold-open pattern interrupt (in media res):** terminal screen recording with a failing build, or founder mid-sentence yelling at a co-founder, then cut to "Let me explain how I got here."
- **Contrarian thesis hook:** "Everyone says you need a niche. After 3 years and 200 newsletter sends, I'm telling you the opposite — and I have the open-rate data."
- **"I almost didn't make this video because…" hook:** Used by Ali Abdaal and Justin Welsh imitators. Works because it primes "this is a rare/honest take." Overused in 2024; in 2026 it's a tell, so use sparingly.
- **Receipts-first hook:** "This is the dashboard. $312k MRR. We went from zero to here in 18 months. I'm going to walk through every channel in order of contribution."
- **Named-experiment hook:** "I tried [thing] for 90 days. Here's the data." This format has fully replaced the 2022–2023 "10x your X in 30 days" promise. Audiences trust experiment-framed over outcome-framed content.

## Title craft (2026)

**Length:** 60–70 characters. YouTube truncates at ~70 in most surfaces; mobile cuts earlier. Frontload the keyword/payload in the first 5 words because mobile sidebars and the watch-page suggested rail truncate aggressively.

**Patterns currently winning:**

- **Numbers + specificity:** "I spent 90 days testing 4 AI agents on real B2B sales calls" beats "How AI is changing sales."
- **Named experiments:** "I tried [X] for [Y] — here's the data."
- **Contrarian/thesis:** "Stop using [popular thing]. Here's what works in 2026."
- **Receipts/dashboards:** "$312k MRR breakdown" / "How a 12-employee company crossed $10M ARR."
- **"Specific Person : Specific Promise"** for founder/expert interviews (Lenny's school).

**A/B testing reality (2026):** YouTube Studio's Test & Compare lets you test **up to three** title/thumbnail combinations, decided by **watch time per impression share** over up to two weeks. Requires advanced features enabled. Doesn't work on Shorts, Premieres, scheduled lives, Made-for-Kids, or age-restricted videos. Thumbnails below 720p get downscaled to 480p, hurting the test. Thumbnail file size cap raised to **50 MB in October 2025** (was 2 MB).

**De-recommended/banned:**

- ALL-CAPS titles.
- More than one emoji.
- "YOU WON'T BELIEVE…" / "I CAN'T BELIEVE…" / "GONE WRONG" — post-2023 clickbait crackdown still actively de-ranks.
- Title that doesn't deliver — Studio serves a "this thumbnail/title may violate our misleading content policy" warning, and repeated misleading thumbnails reduce session and Browse visibility.
- Engagement bait ("LIKE if you agree" in title or pinned comment).

**Honest assessment:** Title A/B testing is high-leverage; thumbnail A/B testing is the highest-leverage single optimization on the platform. Documented case studies show CTR lifts of 30–110% from a single thumbnail swap (Ali Abdaal: ~300k → 1.1M views on one video from a thumbnail change).

## Thumbnail craft

**Specs (unchanged through 2026):** 1280×720, 16:9, JPG/PNG/GIF, ≤50 MB.

**Working formulas:**

- **Face + 2–4-word text overlay + visual element** still dominates, especially for B2B/expert content. Faces with strong emotion lift CTR 20–30%. The face does not need to be the creator's; it can be the guest, an expert being discussed, or a recognizable founder.
- **Text overlay length:** **3–5 words max, 1–2 lines.** Anything longer becomes unreadable at 168×94 px (the actual size in mobile sidebars). Always preview at 25–33% zoom.
- **Color contrast:** high contrast subject vs. background; one accent color used consistently across the channel for brand recognition.
- **MrBeast factory approach** (high-saturation, exaggerated faces, big arrows, before/after split, cluttered density) still has the highest CTRs on entertainment-broad audiences but **performs worse on B2B/expert audiences**, which read it as low-trust.
- **Marques Brownlee minimalism approach** (one product, one color field, one or two words, sometimes no text) wins on tech/B2B/professional audiences. The cleaner pattern winning in 2026 for operator/founder/expert content.
- **Veritasium / Kurzgesagt approach:** custom illustration + a single curiosity-gap label.

**Banned/failing in 2026:**

- More than 1–3 emojis (looks unprofessional, hurts CTR on educational audiences).
- Tiny unreadable text — useless on mobile.
- Misleading thumbnail/title relationship — actively de-ranked.
- "Red circle around random thing" without a real payoff — burned out as a pattern.

## Description craft

**The first 150 characters are the only ones visible above "Show more"** on web and mobile. Treat that as a second hook + keyword payload:

> "I rebuilt our entire onboarding flow in 4 weeks and lifted activation 38%. Full Notion doc, screen recordings, and the 3 mistakes I'd avoid. Tools, links, and timestamps below."

**Structure (top to bottom):**

1. 150-char value statement (visible above the fold).
2. 2–4 paragraphs of context (semantic relevance still helps Search ranking).
3. **Chapters/timestamps** (own block, see below).
4. Mentioned tools/links (3–7 max).
5. Sponsor disclosure with FTC-compliant language.
6. Social/newsletter links (one or two, not a wall).
7. **Three hashtags maximum** (still verified for 2026 — hashtags above three are ignored).

**Description SEO in 2026 has diminished** but is not dead. YouTube primarily indexes **spoken content via transcript** and **title**. Description text adds modest semantic context for the Search system; near-zero effect on Browse. Don't keyword-stuff. Don't paste the same description across videos (the inauthentic-content policy now flags template descriptions on faceless channels).

**Outbound links:** No outright penalty, but **clicks that leave YouTube hurt session contribution**, which is a Browse ranking signal. Put primary links low in the description; use a single newsletter/site link as the primary CTA rather than five competing destinations.

## Chapters / timestamps

**Auto-chapter eligibility (2026, unchanged):**

- First timestamp **must** be `0:00`.
- At least **3 chapters** total.
- Each chapter **at least 10 seconds** long.
- Format: `m:ss`, `mm:ss`, or `hh:mm:ss` followed by a space and the chapter title.
- Each chapter on its own line.

**Optimal chapter count for a 12–15 min B2B video: 5–8 chapters.** Below 5 feels skeletal; above 10 fragments retention.

**2026 reality:** YouTube generates **AI-driven "Key moments"** in search results (since 2023, expanded 2024–2025), and the YouTube app surfaces **AI summaries and chapter-like jumps via Premium/Premium Plus**. User-created chapters still matter for: (1) progress-bar segmentation that lifts retention because viewers self-navigate, (2) appearing as Key Moments in Google Search, (3) accessibility. **Still worth doing, but a smaller marginal lift than in 2022.**

## AI-content disclosure (mandatory compliance)

**Timeline:**

- **Nov 2023:** policy announced.
- **March 2024:** "Altered or synthetic content" toggle live in Studio.
- **May 21, 2025:** disclosure becomes **mandatory** for realistic synthetic/altered audio/video that could mislead.
- **July 15, 2025:** "repetitious content" policy renamed **"inauthentic content"**; YPP eligibility clarified to exclude mass-produced/templated content.
- **2025–2026:** YouTube is carrying forward C2PA 2.1 Content Credentials disclosures automatically; creators using YouTube's own gen-AI tools (Dream Screen, Dream Track) get auto-disclosure. **Cross-platform repost caveat:** when a YouTube long-form is downloaded and reposted to LinkedIn / X / Bluesky / Threads (or excerpted into a short-form clip on those platforms), the JUMBF-encoded C2PA marks are stripped on upload. Each redistribution is a fresh disclosure obligation under EU AI Act Article 50 and California SB 942; see `compliance.md` § "AI-content disclosure regimes" for the hybrid embed + visible-label + sidecar pattern.

**MUST disclose (toggle on at upload):**

- Realistic synthetic voice / cloned voice that sounds real.
- Making a real person appear to say or do something they didn't.
- Altered footage of a real event or place.
- Generating a realistic scene that didn't actually happen.

**Do NOT need to disclose:**

- AI for **production assistance**: scripts, ideas, captions, thumbnails, outlines, infographics.
- Clearly unrealistic content (animation, fantasy, obvious VFX).
- Minor edits: color grading, beauty filters, audio cleanup, background blur.
- Pure AI narration where no real person is being convincingly impersonated (gray area — many creators disclose anyway).

**Penalties:** YouTube can apply a label even when the creator didn't, especially on sensitive topics (elections, health, finance, conflicts, natural disasters). For these topics, the label appears prominently **on the video player itself**. Repeated non-disclosure can result in content removal or YPP suspension.

**"Faceless AI channels" — 2026 reality:** The July 15, 2025 inauthentic-content rule explicitly targeted text-to-speech-only channels with templated formats. **Documented terminations include channels with 80k+ subscribers** (e.g., True Crime Case Files, removed after 150+ AI-narrated videos). **Faceless AI channels can still monetize in 2026 if and only if** each video shows clear human creative input: original commentary, custom research, personal voice, transformative editing, or unique visuals. Slideshow + AI voiceover + stock footage at scale = demonetized. Bruno Sartori (deepfake satire with citations and structure) is the pattern that still works.

**Veo3-generated content:** Surviving where it's used as a clearly stylized or clearly disclosed component, especially with C2PA credentials passed through. Wholesale Veo-generated channels claiming to be real footage are being labeled or removed.

## Monetization (verified 2026)

**YPP eligibility:** 1,000 subs + 4,000 public watch hours in last 12 months **OR** 1,000 subs + 10M Shorts views in last 90 days.

**RPM by category (2025–2026, US-skewing audience):**

- **Personal finance / investing / credit cards:** $10–$30 RPM (top creators $25+; credit-card content can hit $30–$45 CPM in Q4).
- **B2B SaaS / digital marketing / "make money online":** $12–$25 RPM.
- **Tech reviews:** $5–$15 RPM.
- **Education:** $5–$12 RPM ($10–$25 in higher-end sub-niches like exam prep, professional certifications).
- **Health & fitness:** $3–$10 RPM.
- **Gaming:** $1–$5 RPM.
- **Entertainment / vlog:** $2–$8 RPM.
- **Music:** $1–$3 RPM.
- **Shorts:** ~$0.05–$0.30 per 1,000 views in finance, far less in entertainment. **Shorts revenue per-watch-hour reached parity with long-form in the US in October 2025**, but per-view Shorts still earn 50–100× less.

**Mid-roll ad strategy:** Videos must be ≥8 minutes. Place at "natural breakpoints" — between sections, after a payoff, never mid-sentence. Auto-placement improved in 2025 but manual placement still wins on retention. **For a 12-min video, 1–2 well-placed mid-rolls is the maximum** before retention noticeably suffers.

**Reused-content policy (separate from inauthentic):** Compilations, reactions, clip-repackaging, blog-to-video conversions without "significant original commentary" remain ineligible. Applies to **the channel as a whole**, not just individual videos.

## Banned moves and de-rank triggers

- Clickbait that doesn't deliver (de-recommended; repeat offenders get session-level deboost).
- Engagement bait ("comment 'YES' if you agree").
- Misleading thumbnails (Studio warning + de-rank).
- Repurposed/duplicate uploads at scale.
- Comment manipulation (bots, mass-deletion of negative comments).
- Buying views/subs.
- Encouraging subscribers to engage with non-compliant content before it's deleted (called out specifically in the 2025 YPP policy).

## AI-tells / banned phrases (YouTube long-form culture)

The B2B/expert YouTube comment culture in 2026 instantly flags as low-effort or AI-written:

- "In today's fast-paced digital landscape…"
- "Let's dive in" (overused; "let's get into it" mildly more accepted, but minimize either).
- "Buckle up" / "Strap in"
- "Without further ado"
- "Game-changer," "revolutionary," "the holy grail of"
- "You won't believe what happened next"
- "Welcome back to the channel" as the first words (kills the cold open).
- Em-dashes used in spoken voiceover ("It's not just X — it's Y" cadence is now an AI-tell when overused).
- "It's important to note that…"
- "In conclusion" / "To wrap things up"
- AI-flat narration with no inflection on numbers (audiences detect this within seconds).
- Identical pacing on every sentence (giveaway of TTS or unedited AI script).

## Verdict

YouTube long-form is the highest-leverage owned-distribution surface for B2B founders, operators, and experts in 2026. Treat the title and thumbnail as 80% of the work. Treat the cold open as the next 15%. Treat the actual script as the last 5% — but only because the previous 95% is what gets it watched at all.
